Privacy Policy

How this site handles basic usage data and third-party services.

1. Overview

This website ("Molecule Mutation Generator") is an educational tool that allows users to explore the effects of molecular mutations. The site does not intentionally collect personally identifiable information beyond what you voluntarily provide (for example, if you send an email).

2. Log data and analytics

Like many websites, basic technical information may be collected automatically when you visit the site. This can include your browser type, device type, referring page, IP address (anonymized or aggregated where possible), and timestamps. This information is used to understand how the site is used and to maintain and improve the service.

3. Cookies and third-party advertising

This site may use cookies and similar technologies to support basic functionality and, where enabled, to display third-party advertisements (for example, through Google AdSense).

Third-party vendors, including Google, may use cookies to serve ads based on your visits to this and other websites. Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads to you based on your visit to this site and/or other sites on the internet.

You can opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google's Ads Settings or by using other ad-blocking or privacy tools of your choice.

4. Data you submit in the tool

The structures, SMILES strings, and textual descriptions you enter into the Molecule Mutation Generator are processed in order to generate an AI-based response. These inputs may be sent to external AI or hosting providers for computation.

Do not enter confidential, proprietary, or sensitive information into the tool. By using the site, you agree that your inputs may be processed by third-party services as part of generating a response.

5. Email contact

If you contact the site owner by email, your message and email address will be used only to respond to you and manage the correspondence. They will not be sold or shared for marketing purposes.

6. Children's privacy

This site is intended for students and adults with an interest in chemistry and is not directed specifically at children under the age of 13. If you are under your local age of digital consent, use this site under the supervision of a parent, guardian, or teacher.

7. Changes to this policy

This privacy policy may be updated from time to time as the site evolves or as legal requirements change. The "last updated" date may be adjusted when substantial changes are made.
